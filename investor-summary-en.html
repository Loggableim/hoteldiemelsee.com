Investor Summary

Hotel Diemelsee is a small capsule-hotel pilot project in Diemelsee, Germany. The concept targets a clear pricing gap between camping and traditional hotels with a lean 12-capsule setup, low fixed costs and bank-ready project documentation.

Short brief for external partners Three numbers, three arguments, one clear pilot story.

Investment Snapshot

Small, testable pilot before any discussion about expansion.

  • 12 capsules in a compact ground-floor layout.
  • 45,000 EUR capital need with reserve included.
  • 39 EUR base ADR and ~15 % break-even.
Capacity 12 capsules Private sleeping capsules in a compact ground-floor layout.
Capital need 45,000 EUR Including fit-out, safety, systems and reserve.
Base ADR 39 EUR Positioned between camping and standard hotel pricing.
Break-even ~15 % Base-case break-even occupancy level.

Concept and Market Gap

Diemelsee, Willingen and the surrounding outdoor market already generate demand. The missing piece is a standardized, affordable all-weather offer for short stays.

Why the concept is attractive

The pilot avoids typical hotel complexity: no permanent reception, no heavy staffing model and no full-service food operation.

It targets guests who find hotels too expensive and camping too dependent on weather or seasonality. That price gap is the core thesis.

Operations and Economics

The model is built around self-check-in, simple guest rules, standardized cleaning and a small KPI set. The aim is disciplined execution, not scale on day one.

Operations

Lean owner-operated setup

Jan Rahnke is the owner and operator. External partners are used only where specialist support is actually needed.

Demand mix

More than one guest profile

Outdoor travelers, event guests, stopover travelers and budget-conscious solo guests reduce dependence on a single seasonal target group.

Risk control

Small enough to manage, large enough to prove

The pilot size is intentionally limited, allowing the project to validate pricing, occupancy and guest satisfaction before any expansion.
